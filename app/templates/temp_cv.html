Work Examples

  • The Dinosaur that Pooped a Planet; sequel to The Dinosaur that Pooped Christmas by Tom Fletcher and Dougie Poynter and Illustrated by Garry Parsons. This project involved adding animation and interactive features to a illustrated children's book. Largely webkit focused CSS3 and HTML5 for iOS some subtle art modification was needed for it work effectively in this format. (why not the one that won the award you might ask... well this one is better!) https://itunes.apple.com/gb/book/dinosaur-that-pooped-planet/id694729925?mt=11
  • Tree Fu Tom: Tree Fu Go!; a children's book from the BBC series Tree Fu Tom aimed at helping children with developmental coordination disorder and promoting exercise. This project involved character animation and a large amount of interactivity. HTML5, CSS3, and Javascript. Again a large amount artwork modifaction was needed for this format, as well as some creative javascript to work around some of the iOS devices hardware issues. https://itunes.apple.com/gb/book/tree-fu-tom-tree-fu-go!/id588984756?mt=11
  • I Shall Share Midnight; a Social marketing application for Facebook where users could add their profile images to a special edition with a promotional cover for a newly released I Shall Wear Midnight by Terry Pratchett. Flash (AS3), PHP (GD), MySQL, HTML, CSS and Facebook API.
    http://stunjelly.com/blog/i-shall-share-midnight/
  • Beat.js; A method to synchronize timed events across two iframe/windows using localstorage. A common issue (up until recently) with animation for iBooks was that you couldn't animate across pages as the left and right hand pages are loaded independently and at a variable interval depending on content and device. Beat allowed us to do this with creative use of localstorage.
    https://github.com/nicwest/beat
  • 3 seconds mystery; a web marketing project for Marc-antoine Mathieu's 3 Seconds, a graphic novel from the point of view of a beam of light traveling through a story in 3 seconds. The projects goals where a bit open ended so the result was part graphical experiment, part game, and part viral marketing. HTML, CSS, Javascript, PHP, MySQL, OpenID, Facebook/Twitter API's + other social stuff.
    http://3secondsmystery.com/
  • Peter Dickinson (Author Website); website for author Peter Dickinson. Design by Alisa Afkhami. Wordpress, HTML, CSS, Javascript.
    http://peterdickinson.com/
